Felix Riehm

Felix is a computer scientist based in Berlin. His unconventional story began at an early age coding games and other programs in his small bedroom at his parents place. For him coding was always a zen-like experience. Later he moved to Constance to pursue his passion for discovering and learning new ideas. While exploring the scientific horizon at the university, Felix still tried to find new experiences to stay open minded. He joined theater groups, grabbed every opportunity to volunteer and dig deep into literature. His interests in other fields are often reflected in his work. Felix´s work focuses on bringing life into digital experiences by incorporating emotional intelligence and scientific thinking. His latest ambition is to learn Japanese for cross-cultural exchange.

Liebe macht nass
Moosbrugger and Amann are the most powerful family clans in the southern part of Baden in Constance. They dominate life and also hold a 600 years old public dispute.

On the occasion of the 10th anniversary of the thermal bath in Constance, the Amman family hosts a philharmonic charity concert to advertise one of their long-desired project: a concert hall for Constance. No question, once and for all the Amann family wants to make clear that they have the power and influence in their time-honored town.

Naturally, the Moosbruggers are not about to accept this. They go on the warpath and try hard to disrupt the event with everything they got. Their stance on a new concert hall is clear: Who needs a concert hall in this crowded town if we could spend the money for more living space?!

Roman, a young man from the Mossbrugger family and Julia from the Amann family are meeting at the charity event inside the thermal bath. Both, having no clue who they are facing, passionately fall in love with each other not knowing that the feud of their families is dominating the evening.

Erasmus Student Network
The Erasmus Student Network is Europe's largest student organization with sections distributed all over Europe. ESN is a non-profit organization operating on the local, national and international level. As part of this network, ESN Heidelberg was established in 2014 to bring ESN's vision to the beautiful student city of Heidelberg. ESN Heidelberg is therefore part of ESN Germany and part of ESN International.

What we do:
International studies can be a vibrant period of personal discovery and cultural understanding, and our mission is to facilitate and enrich this experience for all international students in Heidelberg. In this context, we organize events, trips, and volunteering opportunities to international students in Heidelberg, and provide help and guidance for newcomers. We strive to enrich international students' experience by helping to integrate them with locals and Heidelberg's international community.

Who we are:
Here in Heidelberg our team consists of German and international students living and promoting the ideas of integration and mobility to other German and international students. We work in close cooperation with the international office of the University of Heidelberg. As Local Erasmus Initiative (LEI) of the University of Heidelberg we are officially supported by the German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD).
